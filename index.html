About

With a passion for seeing projects come into existence, from concept to completed work, Zach C. Gray brings over two decades of experience spanning animation and live action filmmaking. He currently serves as Dean at Southern Adventist University's School of Visual Art and Design. Working alongside Disney veteran director Hendel Butoy, he was instrumental in developing the animation program at Southern in the early 2000s.

Gray's technical credits include work on several high-profile animation projects. He served as Development Technical Artist on the David Animation, Technical Director on the animated Story of Jesus, and a lead developer of the Touchpose animation system for Squarebit Studios, a tool designed to bring greater simplicity to character performance in animation.

On the visual effects side, Gray served as Visual Effects Supervisor and Consulting Director on the 2014 theatrical release Old Fashioned, and supervised a portion of visual effects for the Record Keeper series.

The acting and performance aspects of animation provided a natural bridge to live action directing. Gray co-wrote and directed the family feature Secret of the Cave, recipient of a 2006 Crystal Heart Award at the Heartland Film Festival. Most recently, he returned to live action with the production of Last Light, a short film. His additional credits include production designer and visual effects supervisor across a range of short-form and commercial projects.

Zach earned his M.F.A. in Character Animation from the Savannah College of Art and Design.
